<p>

<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neutron_spin_echo">

 

<b>Neutron spin echo</b></a>

spectroscopy

<big><b> (NSE)</b></big>

is the

<b>only experimental technique</b>

that can determine protein motion on nano length and time scales.

Such motions have long been hypothesized to be the most significant modes for protein function.

We have developed a theoretical framework that allows one to interpret the NSE data.

Our theoretical framework uses simple concepts from

<b>nonequilibrium statistical mechanics</b>,

and does not require elaborate molecular dynamics simulations, complex fits to rotational motion,

or elastic network models. In combination with our experimental methodology of selective <b>deuterium</b>

labeling, our work clears significant hurdles to the understanding of this very important problem.

</p>
