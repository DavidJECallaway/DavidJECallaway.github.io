Henley *David James Edward Callaway* is a biological nanophysicist in the New York University School of Medicine , where he is professor and laboratory director. He was trained as a theoretical physicist by Richard Feynman , Kip Thorne , and Cosmas Zachos , and was previously an associate professor at the Rockefeller University after positions at CERN and Los Alamos National Laboratory .^[/ citation needed /] Callaway's laboratory published about potential therapeutics for Alzheimer's disease based upon apomorphine ^[1] <#cite_note-1> and models of Alzheimer amyloid formation.^[2] <#cite_note-2> He has also studied protein domain dynamics ^[3] <#cite_note-pmid21570668-3> by neutron spin echo spectroscopy, providing a way to observe protein nanomachines in motion.^[4] <#cite_note-4> ^[5] <#cite_note-5> Previous work includes the invention of the microcanonical ensemble approach to lattice gauge theory with Aneesur Rahman ,^[6] <#cite_note-6> ^[7] <#cite_note-7> work on the convexity of the effective potential of quantum field theory ,^[8] <#cite_note-8> work on Langevin dynamics in quantum field theory with John R. Klauder ,^[9] <#cite_note-9> a monograph on quantum triviality ,^[10] <#cite_note-10> constraints on the Higgs boson^[11] <#cite_note-11> and papers on black holes ^[12] <#cite_note-12> and superconductors .^[13] <#cite_note-13> Personal life [edit ] Everest climbers receive Tengboche blessing.
